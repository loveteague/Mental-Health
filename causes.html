What are some causes of global warming?

The burning of fossil fuels has caused warming to pick up significantly more speed in the last century.

Fossil fuels include coal, oil, and natural gas, and burning them causes what is known as the “greenhouse effect” in Earth’s atmosphere.

The sun's rays can enter the atmosphere and cause the greenhouse effect when they reflect off surfaces, preventing the heat from escaping back into space.

The burning of fossil fuels releases gases that keep heat from escaping the atmosphere.

Carbon dioxide, water vapor, methane, nitrous oxide, and chlorofluorocarbons are some examples of these greenhouse gases.

Global warming, as the term implies, is the gradual increase in the average world temperature due to excess heat in the atmosphere.
